History

Dzilam Gonzales is a town located 80 km northeast of the city of Merida, and 10 km from the port of Dzilam de Bravo in Yucatan. This town is commonly known for its various archaeological remains, which unfortunately over the years the volume of these was decreasing. It is also known for its friendly and hard-working people, who work mainly in agriculture and fishing.

Food

There are 3 foods that come to my mind when I think of Dzilam Gonzalez:

Enchiladas campechanas or Chalupas yucatecas

Chalupas yucatecas are made of fried dough that is previously kneaded into a rectangle shape, to which is added handmade tomato sauce and grated cheese, a delight!

Cochinita

Cochinita is very famous in the state of Yucatan, but as every good Yucatecan would say "It tastes better the one from my town".

Archeology

The vestiges of Dzilam are not only composed of the two pyramids or "hills" that most of the inhabitants of this town know, but there are numerous smaller constructions of which there are still remains, sometimes almost imperceptible.

Dzilam is best known in Maya archaeology because of two stelae, stone monuments portraying their rulers, which provide information regarding dates and events related to them. Fray Diego de Landa made mention of numerous stelae in this place, which stood out for their large dimensions

It also has small cenotes that are located both within the town and in the surrounding area.

My brief but wonderful time in Dzilam Gonzalez

After my parents had their first separation, I didn't have a fixed home, I moved house or town constantly whenever there were problems. There were several times that my sister and I stayed in Dzilam Gonzales for a while, however, it wasn't until high school that things seemed to work out; my sister stayed with my mother in Motul (the separation with my sister was very hard) and I stayed in Dzilam Gonzales with my father at my grandparents' house. I usually speak wonders of this town because it was there where I had fantastic experiences, in high school my passion for basketball was born and I was given the opportunity to exploit that passion with a lot of hard work; managing to get with the selection of Yucatan and soon help me financially to pay for my studies at the University. I also met incredible people, with an excellent vibe, I learned to play the guitar, I was even part of a Trio of serenades where I met one of my best friends Eduardo.
